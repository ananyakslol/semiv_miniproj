Mars

About Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, being larger than only Mercury. Mars is often called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish appearance, which is due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface.

Fun Facts

Mars has the largest dust storms in the solar system. They can last for months and cover the entire planet.

Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos, which are small and irregularly shaped. They may be captured asteroids.

Explorations & Discoveries

Mars has been explored more than any other planet beyond Earth. NASA's Perseverance rover landed in February 2021, joining the Curiosity rover which has been operating since 2012. These rovers are searching for signs of ancient microbial life.

The first successful Mars mission was NASA's Mariner 4 flyby in 1965. Since then, numerous orbiters, landers, and rovers have explored the planet, including NASA's InSight lander which has detected marsquakes, providing evidence of seismic activity.

Key Statistics

  • Diameter: 6,779 km
  • Mass: 6.39 × 10^23 kg (0.107 Earth masses)
  • Orbital Period: 687 Earth days
  • Rotation Period: 24.6 hours
  • Average Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km
  • Surface Temperature Range: -153°C to 20°C
