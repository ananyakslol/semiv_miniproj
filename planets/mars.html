Mars

Fourth Planet from the Sun

Overview

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is known as the Red Planet. Its surface is covered in iron oxide dust, giving it a reddish appearance. Mars has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system[3].

Home of Rovers

Mars is the only planet solely inhabited by robots, with several rovers and landers exploring its surface[3].

Composition

Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere, primarily carbon dioxide. Its surface is rocky, with polar ice caps of water and CO₂.

Atmosphere

95% CO₂, 3% N₂, 1.6% Ar, trace O₂ and H₂O

Surface

Basaltic rock, iron oxide dust, polar ice caps

Core

Possibly liquid iron-sulfur core

Fun Facts

Tallest Volcano

Olympus Mons is the tallest volcano in the solar system (21 km high).

Water Ice

There is water ice at the Martian poles and possibly underground.

Dust Storms

Mars has planet-wide dust storms that can last for months.
