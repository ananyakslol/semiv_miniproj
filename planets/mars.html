MARS

Fourth Planet from the Sun

Overview

Mars, often called the "Red Planet," is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest in the solar system. It is located about 227.9 million kilometers from the Sun and is known for its reddish appearance, which results from iron oxide (rust) on its surface. Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere, composed mostly of carbon dioxide, which makes it inhospitable to life as we know it. Despite this, Mars is a primary focus for exploration due to its potential for past or present life and its similarities to Earth. Mars has a day length almost identical to Earth’s, taking 24.6 hours to complete one rotation, but its year lasts almost twice as long, at 687 Earth days. The planet's surface features vast plains, towering volcanoes like Olympus Mons (the largest in the solar system), and the longest canyon, Valles Marineris. Evidence suggests that Mars once had liquid water, and scientists believe it may have supported microbial life in the past. Today, however, water exists mainly as ice, with some trace amounts in the atmosphere. The Martian atmosphere, much thinner than Earth’s, offers little protection from radiation or temperature fluctuations, which can drop to -125°C (-195°F) at night. Despite these harsh conditions, Mars has been a target for numerous space missions, including rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance, which search for signs of ancient life and collect data on the planet’s geology and climate. In recent years, Mars has gained significant interest for potential future human exploration and colonization. Its similarities to Earth, such as its length of day and presence of water ice, make it a prime candidate for the next frontier in space exploration.

My Name?

Mars was named after the Roman god of war due to its reddish appearance, which reminded ancient observers of blood.

Composition

Mars has a thin atmosphere and a dusty, rocky surface. Its reddish color comes from iron oxide (rust) in the soil, and it has a core that may be partially molten.

Atmosphere

95% CO₂, 3% N₂, 1.6% Ar, trace oxygen and water vapor

Surface

Rocky terrain with dust, volcanoes, canyons, and polar ice caps

Core

Iron, sulfur, and nickel core — possibly molten at its center

Fun Facts

Frozen Water

Mars has polar ice caps made of water and carbon dioxide.

Tallest Volcano

Olympus Mons on Mars is the tallest volcano in the solar system.

Dust Storms

Mars experiences planet-wide dust storms that can last for months.
