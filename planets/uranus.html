Uranus

Seventh Planet from the Sun

Overview

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, a cyan-colored ice giant with a unique tilt. Most of the planet is made of water, ammonia, and methane ices, with a complex layered cloud structure[6].

Extreme Tilt

Uranus is tilted over 90°, so it essentially rolls around the Sun on its side[6].

Composition

Uranus is mostly "ices" (water, ammonia, methane) with a small rocky core and a hydrogen-helium atmosphere.

Atmosphere

83% hydrogen, 15% helium, 2% methane

Interior

Water, ammonia, and methane ices

Core

Small rocky core

Exploration

1986

Voyager 2 Flyby

Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to visit Uranus, discovering new moons and rings[6].

Fun Facts

Retrograde Rotation

Uranus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets.

Coldest Planet

Uranus has the coldest atmosphere of any planet in the solar system (-224°C)[6].

Many Moons

Uranus has 27 known moons, named after Shakespearean characters.
