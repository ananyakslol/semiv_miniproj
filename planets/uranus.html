URANUS

Seventh Planet from the Sun

Overview

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is known for its unique tilt, as it rotates on its side relative to its orbit. This tilt gives the planet extreme seasonal variations, with one pole facing the Sun for a portion of its 17.9 Earth-year orbit. Located about 2.87 billion kilometers from the Sun, Uranus is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and water, ammonia, and methane ices. Its blue-green color comes from the methane in its atmosphere, which absorbs red light and reflects blue light. Uranus has a relatively low density compared to the other gas giants, and its atmosphere is divided into two main layers—an upper cloud layer and a lower cloud layer that contains methane ice crystals. The planet experiences extreme winds that can reach speeds of up to 900 kilometers per hour (560 mph), adding to its mysterious nature. Unlike most planets, Uranus has a very weak and irregular magnetic field, likely due to its tilt and internal composition. Uranus has 27 known moons, with the five largest—Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon—named after characters from Shakespeare and Alexander Pope's works. The planet also has a faint ring system, which was discovered in 1977. These rings are much darker than Saturn’s and may consist of ice and dust particles. Exploration of Uranus remains limited, as it has only been visited by Voyager 2 in 1986. However, scientists are keen to understand more about its unusual characteristics, including its extreme tilt, atmospheric composition, and the nature of its ring system.

My Name?

Uranus was named after the ancient Greek god of the sky, making it the only planet with a name from Greek mythology rather than Roman.

Composition

Uranus is an ice giant with a cold atmosphere rich in methane, which gives it a blue-green color. It has icy layers beneath the clouds and a small rocky core.

Atmosphere

Hydrogen, helium, and methane (which absorbs red light and makes it look blue)

Surface

No solid surface — made of icy materials and gas

Core

Rocky core surrounded by water, ammonia, and methane ices

Fun Facts
