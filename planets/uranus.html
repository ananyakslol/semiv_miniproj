Uranus

About Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It has the third-largest planetary radius and fourth-largest planetary mass in the Solar System. Uranus is similar in composition to Neptune, and both have bulk chemical compositions which differ from that of the larger gas giants Jupiter and Saturn.

Fun Facts

Uranus rotates on its side with an axial tilt of 98 degrees. This unique tilt causes the most extreme seasons in the solar system.

Uranus is the only planet named after a Greek deity (Ouranos) rather than a Roman one. It was the first planet discovered in modern times, as it's not visible to the naked eye.

Explorations & Discoveries

Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited Uranus. In January 1986, it flew by the planet, coming within 81,500 km of the cloud tops. During this flyby, Voyager 2 discovered 10 new moons and two new rings.

No other spacecraft has visited Uranus, and there are currently no approved missions to visit the planet in the future, although several mission concepts have been proposed.
