VENUS

Second Planet from the Sun

Overview

Venus is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition, but its conditions are drastically different. Located about 108 million kilometers from the Sun, Venus has a thick atmosphere composed primarily of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid, which create a potent greenhouse effect. This makes Venus the hottest planet in the solar system, with surface temperatures reaching 475°C (880°F), hotter than the surface of Mercury despite being farther from the Sun. Venus has a very slow rotation, taking 243 Earth days to complete one full rotation on its axis. Interestingly, it rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, including Earth, meaning the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east. Its day, however, is shorter than its year—at 225 Earth days for a full orbit around the Sun—leading to unique solar and seasonal cycles. Venus's surface is characterized by vast plains, highland regions, and large volcanoes, with many scientists speculating that volcanic activity might still be occurring today. The planet's extreme heat and thick clouds prevent any direct observations of its surface from Earth, but missions like NASA's Magellan and the Soviet Union's Venera program have provided valuable radar images of its terrain. Despite its inhospitable conditions, Venus remains a subject of great interest for planetary scientists. Studying Venus helps scientists understand the effects of runaway greenhouse gases and offers insights into the potential habitability of exoplanets in other solar systems.

Super-Rotation

Venus's atmosphere has winds over 100 m/s, circling the planet in just 4 Earth days.

Composition

Venus's atmosphere is 96% carbon dioxide, with thick clouds of sulfuric acid. The surface is rocky, with volcanoes and vast plains.

Atmosphere

96% CO₂, 3% N₂, trace gases, sulfuric acid clouds

Surface

Volcanic plains, mountains, and highland regions

Core

Iron-nickel core, similar to Earth

Exploration

1970s-80s

Venera Program

Soviet landers sent back the first images from the surface of Venus.

2006–

Akatsuki

Japan's Akatsuki orbiter studies Venus's atmosphere, weather, and volcanic activity.

Fun Facts

Hottest Planet

Venus is hotter than Mercury due to its greenhouse atmosphere.

Retrograde Rotation

Venus spins backward compared to most planets.

Acid Rain

Its clouds rain sulfuric acid, but the drops evaporate before reaching the ground.
