Venus

About Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is Earth's closest planetary neighbor. It's one of the four inner, terrestrial planets, and it's often called Earth's twin because it's similar in size and density. However, Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide and clouds of sulfuric acid.

Fun Facts

Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, meaning the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east.

A day on Venus lasts longer than a year! It takes 243 Earth days to rotate once on its axis (a day) and 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun (a year).

Explorations & Discoveries

The Soviet Venera program successfully landed probes on Venus in the 1970s and 1980s, providing the first and only images from the surface of Venus. These probes only survived for a short time due to the extreme conditions.

NASA's Magellan mission mapped 98% of Venus's surface using radar between 1990 and 1994. More recently, ESA's Venus Express studied the atmosphere from 2006 to 2014.

Key Statistics

  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Mass: 4.8675 × 10^24 kg (0.815 Earth masses)
  • Orbital Period: 225 Earth days
  • Rotation Period: 243 Earth days (retrograde)
  • Average Distance from Sun: 108.2 million km
  • Surface Temperature: 462°C (hot enough to melt lead)
