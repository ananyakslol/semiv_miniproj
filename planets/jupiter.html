JUPITER

Fifth Planet from the Sun

Overview

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and the fifth planet from the Sun, located about 778 million kilometers away. It is a gas giant composed mostly of hydrogen and helium, with a massive atmosphere that includes powerful storms, including the famous Great Red Spot, a storm that has been raging for at least 400 years. Jupiter’s immense size gives it a powerful gravitational field, which helps to protect the inner planets, including Earth, from potential asteroid and comet impacts. Jupiter's rotation is the fastest of any planet, completing a full spin in just under 10 hours, which contributes to its noticeable oblate shape—meaning it is slightly flattened at the poles and bulging at the equator. A day on Jupiter is very short compared to Earth’s, but its year lasts nearly 12 Earth years, as it takes that long to complete an orbit around the Sun. Jupiter has 79 known moons, with the four largest being Ganymede, Callisto, Io, and Europa, collectively known as the Galilean moons. These moons are fascinating in their own right, with Europa believed to have an ocean beneath its icy crust that may harbor conditions suitable for life. Jupiter also has a faint ring system, which is less prominent than Saturn’s but still an important feature in its complex system of moons and rings. Jupiter’s powerful magnetosphere extends far beyond its moons, creating intense radiation belts that make exploration challenging but also provide clues about planetary magnetic fields and their origins. Ongoing missions, such as NASA’s Juno spacecraft, continue to provide valuable data on Jupiter’s atmosphere, composition, and magnetic environment.

My Name?

Mars was named after the Roman god of war due to its reddish appearance, which reminded ancient observers of blood.

Composition

Mars has a thin atmosphere and a dusty, rocky surface. Its reddish color comes from iron oxide (rust) in the soil, and it has a core that may be partially molten.

Atmosphere

95% CO₂, 3% N₂, 1.6% Ar, trace oxygen and water vapor

Surface

Rocky terrain with dust, volcanoes, canyons, and polar ice caps

Core

Iron, sulfur, and nickel core — possibly molten at its center
