Jupiter

Fifth Planet from the Sun

Overview

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the solar system, more than twice as massive as all other planets combined. It is a gas giant with a thick atmosphere and dozens of moons[4].

Great Red Spot

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a giant storm larger than Earth, raging for centuries.

Composition

Jupiter is made mostly of hydrogen and helium, with a possible rocky core deep inside. Its atmosphere features colorful bands and storms.

Atmosphere

~90% hydrogen, ~10% helium, trace methane, ammonia, water

Core

Possible rock/metal core, surrounded by metallic hydrogen

Clouds

Ammonia ice, water ice, and other compounds

Exploration

1973–

Pioneer, Voyager, Galileo

Early flybys and the Galileo orbiter revealed Jupiter's atmosphere and moons.

2016–

Juno Orbiter

Juno studies Jupiter's atmosphere, gravity, and magnetic field[4].

2024–

Europa Clipper

Launched to study Jupiter's moon Europa and its potential for life[4].

Fun Facts

Many Moons

Jupiter has at least 95 known moons, including Ganymede, the largest in the solar system[4].

Fast Spinner

Jupiter has the shortest day: about 10 hours long.

Biggest Planet

Over 1,000 Earths could fit inside Jupiter.
