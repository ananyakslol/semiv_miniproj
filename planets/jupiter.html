Jupiter

About Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined, but slightly less than one-thousandth the mass of the Sun.

Fun Facts

Jupiter has the shortest day of all the planets. It rotates on its axis once every 9 hours and 55 minutes.

The Great Red Spot is a persistent high-pressure region in Jupiter's atmosphere, producing an anticyclonic storm that is larger than Earth. It has been continuously observed since 1830.

Explorations & Discoveries

NASA's Juno spacecraft entered orbit around Jupiter in 2016 and continues to study the planet's composition, gravity field, magnetic field, and polar magnetosphere.

Earlier missions to Jupiter include Pioneer 10 and 11, Voyager 1 and 2, Galileo, Cassini, and New Horizons, all of which have helped scientists understand this massive planet.
