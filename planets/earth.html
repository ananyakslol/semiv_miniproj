Earth

Third Planet from the Sun

Overview

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only planet known to harbor life. Earth formed over 4.5 billion years ago, along with the sun.

Earth's gravity interacts with other objects in space, especially the Moon, which is Earth's only natural satellite. Earth orbits around the Sun in 365.256 days, a period known as an Earth sidereal year.

The Blue Planet

Earth is often referred to as the "Blue Planet" due to its abundance of water on its surface. About 71% of Earth's surface is covered with water, mostly by oceans.

Composition

Earth's interior remains active with a solid iron inner core, a liquid outer core that generates Earth's magnetic field, and a convecting mantle that drives plate tectonics.

Fun Facts

Highest Point

Mount Everest reaches 8,848 meters (29,029 ft) above sea level.

Deepest Point

Mariana Trench reaches 10,994 meters (36,070 ft) below sea level.

Lightning Strikes

About 100 lightning bolts strike Earth's surface every second.

Biodiversity

Earth is home to an estimated 8.7 million species, with only 1.2 million identified.

Did You Know?

Earth is the only planet not named after a god. The name comes from the Old English word "eorthe" and the Anglo-Saxon word "erda," which means ground or soil.
