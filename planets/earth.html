EARTH

Third Planet from the Sun

Overview

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life, a distinction it owes to its stable atmosphere, presence of liquid water, and moderate temperatures. Located about 150 million kilometers from the Sun, Earth orbits in the habitable zone, where conditions are just right for water to exist in its liquid form, which is essential for life as we know it. The atmosphere is composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace amounts of other gases, making it suitable for sustaining life. Earth’s unique axial tilt gives rise to the changing seasons, with the Northern and Southern Hemispheres experiencing opposite seasons. The planet also has a relatively stable climate compared to other planets, thanks to the balance between the Sun’s energy and Earth’s ability to radiate heat into space. Earth’s surface is about 71% water, with oceans, rivers, and lakes covering most of the planet, and the remaining 29% consists of landmasses that include continents and islands. The planet has a diverse range of ecosystems, from tropical rainforests to arid deserts, supporting a vast variety of life forms. Earth's geologic activity, including tectonic plate movements, volcanoes, and earthquakes, constantly reshapes the surface, contributing to its dynamic environment. This ongoing process helps in the recycling of minerals and gases essential for life. Earth’s moon, the only natural satellite in our solar system, plays an important role in stabilizing the planet's rotation and supporting life. Its gravitational pull influences the tides, weather patterns, and even the tilt of Earth’s axis, which is essential for the planet’s long-term climate stability.

My Name?

Earth is the only planet not named after a Greek or Roman deity. Its name comes from Old English and Germanic words meaning “ground” or “soil.”

Composition

Earth has a balanced structure that supports life: a breathable atmosphere, a solid surface with water and land, and a dense core that generates a magnetic field.

Atmosphere

78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, 1% other gases (argon, CO₂, etc.)

Surface

Continents, oceans, forests, mountains, deserts, and polar ice caps

Core

Solid inner core and molten outer core of iron and nickel

Fun Facts

Magnetic Shield

Earth’s magnetic field, created by its spinning iron core, helps protect us from solar wind and cosmic radiation

Synchronised Spin

The Moon always shows the same face to Earth — it’s tidally locked, meaning its rotation period matches its orbit.

One Moon

Earth has one natural satellite — the Moon, which affects tides and stabilizes our tilt.
