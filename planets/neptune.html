Neptune

About Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in the Solar System. It is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet. Neptune is 17 times the mass of Earth, and slightly more massive than its near-twin Uranus.

Fun Facts

Neptune has the strongest winds in the Solar System, reaching speeds of up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph).

Neptune was not discovered by telescopic observation but through mathematical prediction. Irregularities in Uranus's orbit led astronomers to predict Neptune's existence and location.

Explorations & Discoveries

Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited Neptune. In August 1989, it flew by the planet, passing about 4,950 km above Neptune's north pole. This flyby provided the first close-up images of Neptune and its moons.

During its Neptune encounter, Voyager 2 discovered six new moons, four new rings, and a "Great Dark Spot" similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot. The Great Dark Spot has since disappeared, but new ones have formed, showing the dynamic nature of Neptune's atmosphere.
