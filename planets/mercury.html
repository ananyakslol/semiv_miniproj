MERCURY

First Planet from the Sun

Overview

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in our solar system. Because of its proximity to the Sun, it experiences extreme variations in surface temperature, although it surprisingly has no atmosphere to trap heat. This means its surface can be scorching hot during the day and freezing cold at night. Despite being so close to the Sun, Mercury isn’t the hottest planet — that title belongs to Venus due to its thick atmosphere. One of the most unique things about Mercury is its orbit. It moves around the Sun faster than any other planet, completing a full orbit in just 88 Earth days. However, its rotation is very slow — one day on Mercury (from one sunrise to the next) takes about 176 Earth days! This creates very long days and nights, which contributes to its dramatic temperature swings. Mercury's surface is covered in craters, similar to the Moon. These were formed by collisions with meteoroids and comets over billions of years. Because it has almost no atmosphere to erode or weather these marks, many of the craters are still clearly visible, making Mercury a kind of fossil record of the early solar system. Interestingly, Mercury has a relatively large iron core for its size, which generates a weak magnetic field — something unexpected for such a small, rocky world. Scientists believe this may offer clues to how Mercury formed and why it differs from other inner planets. Although Mercury has no moons or rings, it remains one of the most mysterious and extreme planets in our solar system.

My Name?

Mercury was named after the Roman god Mercury, the swift messenger of the gods, because the planet moves quickly across the sky.

Composition

Mercury is a dense, rocky planet with a large metal core and a very thin atmosphere. Its surface is heavily cratered and similar in appearance to the Moon.

Atmosphere

Very thin exosphere of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium

Surface

Rocky, cratered plains, cliffs, and ancient impact basins

Core

Large iron core — makes up about 85% of the planet’s radius

Exploration

1970s-80s

Venera Program

Soviet landers sent back the first images from the surface of Venus.

2006–

Akatsuki

Japan's Akatsuki orbiter studies Venus's atmosphere, weather, and volcanic activity.

Fun Facts

Fastest Planet

Mercury orbits the Sun in just 88 Earth days — the shortest orbit in the solar system.

Extreme Temperatures

It can go from 430°C during the day to -180°C at night because it has almost no atmosphere.

No Moon

Mercury has no moons or rings — it's just too close to the Sun.
