MERCURY

First Planet from the Sun

Overview

Mercury, the smallest planet in our solar system, is the closest to the Sun, orbiting at a distance of approximately 57.9 million kilometers. Despite its proximity to the Sun, Mercury experiences extreme temperature fluctuations due to its lack of atmosphere. During the day, temperatures soar up to 430°C (800°F), while at night, they plummet to -180°C (-290°F). This stark temperature contrast is a result of its inability to trap heat, given its thin exosphere, which is too sparse to act as a protective barrier. Mercury's orbital period is the shortest in the solar system, taking just 88 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun. As a result, a year on Mercury is much shorter than on Earth, while a day on Mercury, defined as the time it takes for the planet to rotate once on its axis, lasts about 59 Earth days. Its slow rotation combined with its elliptical orbit contributes to its intense temperature swings and creates unique day-night cycles. The planet’s surface resembles that of the Moon, with numerous impact craters from collisions with asteroids and comets. These craters are thought to have formed over billions of years, with little geological activity to alter their appearance. The lack of atmosphere means that there is no erosion, and the surface remains almost unchanged. The planet's small size also means that it has a weak gravitational field, making it difficult to retain any significant atmosphere or water. Although it is challenging to study Mercury from Earth due to its proximity to the Sun, spacecraft missions like NASA's Messenger have provided valuable insights into the planet’s characteristics. Mercury’s extreme conditions and mysterious features continue to intrigue scientists, offering key clues about the formation and evolution of the inner planets in our solar system.

My Name?

Mercury was named after the Roman god Mercury, the swift messenger of the gods, because the planet moves quickly across the sky.

Composition

Mercury is a dense, rocky planet with a large metal core and a very thin atmosphere. Its surface is heavily cratered and similar in appearance to the Moon.

Atmosphere

Very thin exosphere of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium

Surface

Rocky, cratered plains, cliffs, and ancient impact basins

Core

Large iron core — makes up about 85% of the planet’s radius

Akatsuki

Fun Facts

Fastest Orbit

Mercury completes one trip around the Sun in just 88 Earth days.

Huge Temperature Swings

Temperatures can swing from 430°C during the day to -180°C at night.

No Moons

Mercury has no moons or rings — it's just too close to the Sun.
