Mercury

First Planet from the Sun

Overview

Mercury is the first planet from the Sun and the smallest in the Solar System. It is named after the Roman god Mercurius. Mercury is a rocky, heavily cratered world with a very thin atmosphere and extreme temperature variations.

Extreme Temperatures

Mercury experiences the largest temperature swing in the solar system, from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.

Composition

Mercury has a large metallic core, making up about 85% of its radius, surrounded by a silicate mantle and crust. Its surface is covered in craters and smooth plains.

Core

Iron-nickel core, partially molten

Mantle & Crust

Silicate rock, heavily cratered

Atmosphere

Extremely thin, mostly oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, potassium

Exploration

1974-75

Mariner 10

First spacecraft to visit Mercury, mapping about 45% of its surface.

2011-2015

MESSENGER

Orbited Mercury, mapped the entire surface, discovered water ice at poles, and crashed into the planet in 2015[1].

2018–2026

BepiColombo

Joint ESA/JAXA mission launched in 2018, will orbit Mercury in 2026 to study its magnetosphere and surface in detail[1].

Fun Facts

Closest to the Sun

Despite being closest to the Sun, Mercury is not the hottest planet (Venus is hotter).

Long Days

One day on Mercury (sunrise to sunrise) lasts 176 Earth days!

Heavy Metal

Mercury has the highest density of any planet except Earth.
