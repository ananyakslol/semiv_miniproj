Mercury

About Mercury

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. It has no natural satellites and is one of four terrestrial planets in our solar system. Mercury has a solid surface that is covered with craters like our Moon.

Fun Facts

A year on Mercury lasts only 88 Earth days, but a day on Mercury lasts 176 Earth days – meaning a day on Mercury is twice as long as its year!

Mercury has the most eccentric orbit of all the planets, and the smallest axial tilt at just 0.034 degrees.

Explorations & Discoveries

The first spacecraft to visit Mercury was NASA's Mariner 10, which flew by the planet three times in 1974 and 1975, mapping about 45% of the surface.

NASA's MESSENGER spacecraft orbited Mercury from 2011 to 2015, providing the first complete maps of Mercury's surface and discovering evidence of water ice in permanently shadowed craters at the poles.

Key Statistics

  • Diameter: 4,879 km
  • Mass: 3.3011 × 10^23 kg (0.055 Earth masses)
  • Orbital Period: 88 Earth days
  • Rotation Period: 58.6 Earth days
  • Average Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km
  • Surface Temperature Range: -173°C to 427°C
