SATURN

Sixth Planet from the Sun

Overview

Saturn, the sixth planet from the Sun, is the second largest planet in the solar system and is most famous for its stunning system of rings. Located about 1.43 billion kilometers from the Sun, Saturn is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. Its rings, made of ice particles and dust, are its most distinguishing feature and are visible even through small telescopes. These rings extend thousands of kilometers from the planet but are only a few hundred meters thick. A day on Saturn is only about 10.7 hours long, but its year lasts nearly 30 Earth years, making it one of the longest years in the solar system. Saturn’s atmosphere is primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, and traces of methane, with strong winds and storms that can reach speeds of up to 1,800 kilometers per hour (1,100 mph). The planet has a distinctive yellowish hue due to the presence of ammonia clouds in its atmosphere. Saturn has 83 confirmed moons, with Titan being the largest and most fascinating. Titan is the second-largest moon in the solar system and has a thick atmosphere composed of nitrogen, with lakes and rivers of liquid methane. Scientists are keen to explore Titan further, as its conditions may resemble those of early Earth. The planet’s magnetic field is 578 times stronger than Earth’s, and its powerful gravity is a key factor in maintaining its iconic ring system. Saturn has been the target of multiple space missions, including NASA’s Cassini mission, which spent 13 years studying the planet, its rings, and its moons.

My Name?

Saturn was named after the Roman god of agriculture and time, and he was also the father of Jupiter in mythology.

Composition

Saturn, another gas giant, has a thick atmosphere of hydrogen and helium with fast-moving winds. Its interior is made of compressed gas and a small core.

Atmosphere

96% hydrogen, 3% helium, trace gases including methane and ammonia

Surface

No true surface — visible “surface” is upper cloud layers

Core

Dense core of rock and ice, surrounded by metallic hydrogen

Fun Facts

Iconic Rings

Saturn’s rings are made of ice and rock and stretch over 280,000 km wide.

Fast Winds

Winds on Saturn can reach speeds of 1,800 km/h.

Low Density

Saturn is so light it would float in water (if you could find a big enough tub!).
