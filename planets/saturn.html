Saturn

Sixth Planet from the Sun

Overview

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the solar system, famous for its stunning rings. It is a gas giant with a pale yellow hue due to ammonia crystals in its atmosphere[5].

Iconic Rings

Saturn's rings are made mostly of ice particles, with some rock and dust[5].

Composition

Saturn is mostly hydrogen and helium, with a rocky core, metallic hydrogen layer, and an outer layer of molecular hydrogen.

Atmosphere

96% hydrogen, 3% helium, trace methane, ammonia, water vapor

Core

Rocky core surrounded by metallic hydrogen

Rings

Ice, rock, and dust particles

Exploration

1979–

Pioneer, Voyager, Cassini

Voyager and Cassini provided detailed images and data about Saturn and its rings[5].

2004–2017

Cassini Orbiter

Cassini orbited Saturn for 13 years, studying its rings, atmosphere, and moons.

Fun Facts

Would Float

Saturn is less dense than water and would float in a giant bathtub!

Many Moons

Saturn has at least 274 moons and moonlets, including Titan, the second-largest moon in the solar system[5].

Super Winds

Winds on Saturn can reach 1,800 km/h (1,100 mph).
